01 · Scholarship

Nonprofit Organizations & Urban Governance

How governments deploy nonprofits to manage urban poverty — the political economy of social-service contracting, the micro-relations of governance linking city hall to neighborhood organizations, and the rise and costs of evidence-based evaluation mandates in the nonprofit sector.

How do governments govern through nonprofits? Who gets funded, and where? What do RCT mandates do to nonprofits? How do politics and service provision intertwine?

02 · Scholarship

Internet Innovation

The University of Chicago Internet Innovation Initiative uses novel measurement methods to document the digital divide in Chicago and other cities — examining disparities in internet access, affordability, and performance across race and income, and the politics of federal broadband policy.

Who has fast, affordable internet — and who doesn't? How should we measure internet performance? Are federal broadband maps accurate? How do communities connect themselves?

03 · Collaboration

AI Innovation in Markets & Governance

An interdisciplinary University of Chicago collaboration investigating how artificial intelligence challenges the rules and practices of governance — and what the consequences are for innovation and risk. The group spans the Crown Family School, Law School, Booth School of Business, Harris School of Public Policy, the Social Sciences Division, and Computer Science.

How does AI challenge governance? Who bears AI's risks and rewards? How should markets and institutions adapt?
