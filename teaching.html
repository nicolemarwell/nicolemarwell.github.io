In the classroom

Teaching & Mentoring

Graduate and undergraduate teaching on cities, organizations, social policy, and the social sector — and doctoral students placed across sociology, social work, and public policy.

University of Chicago

Current courses

Doctoral · Crown Family School
Communities, Organizations, and Democracy: Key Challenges in Urban Governance
A doctoral seminar developing theoretical tools to think about cities at the organizational level — communities, community organizations, and public bureaucracies.
Master's · Crown Family School
Key Issues in Social Sector Governance
Key concepts for thinking about the social sector: public policy, markets, organizational practice, cultural beliefs, and individual action.
Master's · Crown Family School
Social Intervention: Policies and Programs I & II
Contemporary social-welfare policy for low-income families, and the analytic frameworks linking social problems, policies, and the practices that result.
Master's · Crown Family School
Organizational Theory and Analysis for Human Services
Applying diverse organizational theories and perspectives to the analysis of social-service organizations.
Graduate · with Harris School
Big Data and Public Policy
The conceptual underpinnings of data-science and social-science approaches to policy analysis.
Undergraduate · Crown Family School
How Things Get Done in Cities and Why
Interest representation, decision-making, and inclusion/exclusion in the contemporary city, drawing on multiple disciplines.
