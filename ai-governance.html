AI Innovation in Markets and Governance

An interdisciplinary collaboration at the University of Chicago investigating how artificial intelligence challenges the rules and practices of governance, and what the consequences are for innovation and risk.

About

The Group

The AI Innovation in Markets and Governance group brings together researchers from across the University of Chicago to examine one of the central challenges of our time: how should governance respond to AI, and how does AI transform the systems through which markets and institutions operate?

The group takes an interdisciplinary approach, combining perspectives from sociology, law, economics, political science, and computer science. Research questions span regulatory design, algorithmic accountability, the distribution of AI-generated value and risk, and the organizational and political dynamics of AI adoption in public and private institutions.
